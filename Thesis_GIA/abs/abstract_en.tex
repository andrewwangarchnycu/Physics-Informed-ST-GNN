\begin{abstract}

Taiwan's urban environments lie in a subtropical hot-humid climate, where high summer humidity suppresses evaporative cooling and drives outdoor pedestrian thermal stress to persistently uncomfortable, and at times hazardous, levels. However, the computational latency of high-fidelity numerical simulation (such as Computational Fluid Dynamics and Ladybug Tools) confines microclimate assessment to post-hoc verification rather than early-stage design iteration; prevailing morphological optimization targets a global spatial average and overlooks pedestrians' dynamic thermal exposure along their routes; and model training data have largely relied on procedurally generated geometry and typical-year weather, lacking external validity in real environments. To address these gaps, this study develops a generative urban design optimization framework that integrates a Physics-Informed Spatio-Temporal Graph Neural Network (PI-ST-GNN), a thermal-comfort walkway evaluation, and the Non-dominated Sorting Genetic Algorithm II (NSGA-II). Buildings and air-evaluation points are encoded as distinct node types in a heterogeneous graph; a Relational Graph Convolutional Network with a Long Short-Term Memory encoder captures the geometry–time–heat-stress mapping under physics-informed soft constraints, replacing costly numerical simulation to predict the full-field, hourly Universal Thermal Climate Index (UTCI).

To strengthen the surrogate model's real-world credibility, the training data were constructed entirely from real geographic environments: real OpenStreetMap building footprints and heights, ETH global canopy heights for vegetation shading, and hourly air temperature and relative humidity measured by the MOENV IoT station network. From these, 300 real scenes—each simultaneously satisfying criteria of real buildings, land-cover heterogeneity, a real IoT station, and real canopy—were selected across the Hsinchu, Taoyuan, Miaoli, and Taichung region, with microclimate simulation performed by the official Radiance/EnergyPlus engine rather than a custom physics approximation. On held-out, unseen real test scenes, the model attained a coefficient of determination of $R^2 = 0.9753$ and a root-mean-square error of $0.83\,^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$, indicating good generalization to the spatial heat-stress structure imposed by real urban geometry. The predicted field then drives thermal-exposure-weighted walkway evaluation and NSGA-II multi-objective morphological optimization, jointly producing optimized building configurations and thermally comfortable walking paths. The contribution of this study lies in anchoring the surrogate model's training in real geospatial data and measured meteorology, and in integrating full-field prediction, walkway thermal-comfort evaluation, and generative optimization into an interactive, closed-loop design-decision workflow for climate-adaptive urban design.

Keywords: Graph Neural Network, Urban Microclimate, Universal Thermal Climate Index, Thermal-Comfort Walkway, Multi-Objective Optimization, Generative Design

\end{abstract}
